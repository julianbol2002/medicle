CASE_ID: 001
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: cardiology

DIAGNOSIS:
Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy

ALIASES:
HCM
hypertrophic obstructive cardiomyopathy
HOCM

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A young adult presents to the emergency department after a brief loss of consciousness during exertion, with no preceding chest pain.
2. He reports occasional palpitations over the past year and has been generally well otherwise.
3. A male relative died suddenly in his early forties, with no diagnosis ever established.
4. Cardiac auscultation reveals a systolic murmur at the left lower sternal border that becomes louder when he stands from a squatting position.
5. ECG shows increased voltage in the precordial leads with deep narrow Q waves in the lateral leads, and echocardiography reveals asymmetric thickening of the interventricular septum with systolic anterior motion of the mitral valve.
6. He is started on a non-vasodilating beta-blocker, advised to avoid competitive sports, and referred for genetic counseling and ICD evaluation.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- HCM is the most common cause of sudden cardiac death in young athletes.
- The murmur increases with maneuvers that decrease preload (Valsalva, standing) and decreases with squatting.
- Most cases are autosomal dominant mutations in sarcomere genes such as MYH7 and MYBPC3.

AS

ALIASES:
pericardial tamponade
tamponade

VIGNETTE_LINES:
