3. Cardiac examination reveals a soft early diastolic sound and mild bibasilar crackles.
4. CBC shows normocytic anemia, and erythrocyte sedimentation rate is elevated.
5. Transthoracic echocardiography demonstrates a mobile pedunculated mass in the left atrium that prolapses through the mitral valve during diastole.
6. Surgical excision specimen reveals stellate cells embedded in a myxoid stroma, confirming a cardiac myxoma.

TEACHING_POINTS:

* Positional dyspnea or presyncope with constitutional symptoms should raise suspicion for an intracardiac obstructive lesion.
* Atrial myxomas can mimic valvular disease, heart failure, or endocarditis depending on their size and mobility.
* Elevated inflammatory markers and anemia may be paraneoplastic features rather than evidence of infection.
* Echocardiography is the key imaging study, but pathology confirms the tumor type after resection.
