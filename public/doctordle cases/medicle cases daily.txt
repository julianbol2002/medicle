CASE_ID: 001
DIFFICULTY: hard
SYSTEM: cardiology

DIAGNOSIS:
Cardiac amyloidosis

ALIASES:
Amyloid cardiomyopathy
Infiltrative amyloid heart disease
Restrictive cardiomyopathy due to amyloidosis

VIGNETTE_LINES:

1. A 68-year-old man presents with progressive shortness of breath.
2. He has had several months of bilateral hand numbness and underwent carpal tunnel release 2 years ago.
3. Examination shows elevated jugular venous pressure, bilateral lower-extremity edema, and clear lungs.
4. ECG demonstrates low-voltage QRS complexes, and basic metabolic panel shows mild renal insufficiency.
5. Echocardiography reveals concentric ventricular wall thickening with biatrial enlargement and a restrictive filling pattern despite a preserved ejection fraction.
6. Endomyocardial biopsy demonstrates extracellular amorphous deposits with apple-green birefringence on Congo red staining.

TEACHING_POINTS:

* Heart failure symptoms with increased ventricular wall thickness and unexpectedly low ECG voltage should suggest an infiltrative cardiomyopathy.
* Bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome and peripheral neuropathy can precede overt cardiac manifestations of amyloid disease.
* Restrictive physiology often presents with prominent right-sided congestion and relatively clear lungs early in the course.
* Tissue confirmation relies on identifying amyloid deposits with Congo red staining and characteristic birefringence.

CASE_ID: 002
DIFFICULTY: hard
SYSTEM: pulmonology

DIAGNOSIS:
Allergic bronchopulmonary aspergillosis

ALIASES:
ABPA
Allergic Aspergillus airway disease
Aspergillus hypersensitivity lung disease

VIGNETTE_LINES:

1. A 29-year-old woman presents with worsening cough.
2. She has a long history of asthma requiring frequent rescue inhaler use.
3. Examination reveals diffuse expiratory wheezing and scattered crackles.
4. CBC shows peripheral eosinophilia, and chest radiography demonstrates transient patchy upper-lobe opacities.
5. CT of the chest shows central bronchiectasis with mucus plugging.
6. Serum testing demonstrates markedly elevated Aspergillus-specific IgE levels with an immediate hypersensitivity response to Aspergillus antigen.

TEACHING_POINTS:

* Worsening asthma with eosinophilia and fleeting pulmonary infiltrates should prompt consideration of a hypersensitivity process rather than simple infection.
* Central bronchiectasis is a key imaging clue that helps narrow the differential in difficult asthma cases.
* Patients may produce thick mucus plugs and be misdiagnosed with recurrent bacterial pneumonia.
* Confirmation relies on evidence of Aspergillus sensitization in the correct clinical setting rather than invasive fungal infection.

CASE_ID: 003
DIFFICULTY: hard
SYSTEM: gastroenterology

DIAGNOSIS:
Primary sclerosing cholangitis

ALIASES:
PSC
Chronic fibrosing cholangitis
Sclerosing cholangitis

VIGNETTE_LINES:

1. A 34-year-old man presents with progressive fatigue.
2. He has a several-year history of intermittent bloody diarrhea treated intermittently with mesalamine.
3. Physical examination shows mild scleral icterus and right upper quadrant tenderness.
4. Liver studies reveal a cholestatic pattern with elevated alkaline phosphatase and bilirubin.
5. Magnetic resonance cholangiopancreatography shows multifocal short strictures and segmental dilatations involving the intrahepatic and extrahepatic bile ducts.
6. Brush cytology obtained during endoscopic evaluation shows no malignancy, and the cholangiographic pattern is diagnostic of chronic fibrosing inflammation of the biliary tree.

TEACHING_POINTS:

* Cholestatic liver enzyme elevation in a patient with inflammatory bowel disease should raise concern for a biliary tract disorder.
* The associated colitis is classically ulcerative colitis, but hepatobiliary disease activity does not necessarily parallel bowel symptoms.
* Multifocal stricturing and beading of the biliary tree help distinguish this condition from isolated obstruction or primary biliary disease.
* Patients require surveillance because of increased risk for cholangiocarcinoma and colorectal neoplasia.

CASE_ID: 004
DIFFICULTY: hard
SYSTEM: endocrinology

DIAGNOSIS:
Primary hyperaldosteronism

ALIASES:
Conn syndrome
Aldosterone-producing adenoma syndrome
Primary aldosteronism

VIGNETTE_LINES:

1. A 41-year-old woman presents with muscle weakness.
2. She has had difficult-to-control hypertension despite taking three antihypertensive medications.
3. Examination shows no peripheral edema and normal volume status.
4. Basic metabolic panel demonstrates hypokalemia and mild metabolic alkalosis.
5. Plasma studies show a suppressed renin level with an elevated aldosterone-to-renin ratio, and CT demonstrates a unilateral adrenal nodule.
6. Adrenal venous sampling shows markedly increased aldosterone secretion from the left adrenal gland compared with the right.

TEACHING_POINTS:

* Resistant hypertension with unexplained hypokalemia should prompt evaluation for excess mineralocorticoid activity.
* Lack of edema despite sodium retention is explained by aldosterone escape and pressure natriuresis.
* An elevated aldosterone-to-renin ratio narrows the differential but does not localize the source.
* Adrenal venous sampling is the confirmatory test used to distinguish unilateral from bilateral disease before surgery.

CASE_ID: 005
DIFFICULTY: hard
SYSTEM: neurology

DIAGNOSIS:
Normal pressure hydrocephalus

ALIASES:
NPH
Communicating hydrocephalus of adulthood
Hakim-Adams syndrome

VIGNETTE_LINES:

1. A 76-year-old man presents with progressive difficulty walking.
2. His family reports several months of increasing forgetfulness and urinary urgency.
3. Examination shows a broad-based, magnetic gait with difficulty initiating steps.
4. MRI of the brain demonstrates ventriculomegaly out of proportion to cortical atrophy.
5. After a large-volume lumbar puncture, his gait speed improves noticeably over the next several hours.
6. Continuous cerebrospinal fluid drainage testing reproduces marked symptomatic improvement, supporting benefit from ventriculoperitoneal shunting.

TEACHING_POINTS:

* Gait impairment often appears before cognitive decline and incontinence in this potentially reversible cause of dementia.
* Ventriculomegaly alone is not sufficient, because cerebral atrophy can also enlarge the ventricles.
* Improvement after CSF removal helps distinguish this condition from degenerative parkinsonian and Alzheimer-spectrum disorders.
* Early recognition matters because shunting can significantly improve function, especially gait.

CASE_ID: 006
DIFFICULTY: hard
SYSTEM: nephrology

DIAGNOSIS:
Thin basement membrane nephropathy

ALIASES:
Benign familial hematuria
Thin basement membrane disease
Familial thin GBM nephropathy

VIGNETTE_LINES:

1. A 22-year-old woman presents with recurrent dark urine.
2. She reports that several relatives were told they had blood in the urine but never developed kidney failure.
3. Examination is unremarkable without edema, hearing loss, or visual abnormalities.
4. Urinalysis shows persistent microscopic hematuria with minimal proteinuria and a normal serum creatinine level.
5. Kidney biopsy shows diffuse thinning of the glomerular basement membrane on electron microscopy without immune complex deposition.
6. Genetic testing identifies a heterozygous collagen IV abnormality consistent with an inherited thin glomerular basement membrane disorder.

TEACHING_POINTS:

* Persistent isolated hematuria in a young patient with preserved kidney function and family history suggests a hereditary basement membrane abnormality.
* The absence of significant proteinuria, deafness, and ocular findings helps distinguish this disorder from Alport spectrum disease.
* Light microscopy may be nondiagnostic, making electron microscopy especially useful in evaluation.
* Most patients have a benign course, although follow-up is warranted if proteinuria or hypertension develops.

CASE_ID: 007
DIFFICULTY: hard
SYSTEM: hematology_oncology

DIAGNOSIS:
Paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria

ALIASES:
PNH
Complement-mediated hemolytic anemia due to GPI-anchor deficiency
Marchiafava-Micheli syndrome

VIGNETTE_LINES:

1. A 31-year-old man presents with fatigue.
2. He reports episodic cola-colored urine in the morning and was treated for an unexplained abdominal vein thrombosis last year.
3. Examination shows conjunctival pallor and mild scleral icterus.
4. Laboratory studies reveal normocytic anemia, elevated lactate dehydrogenase, low haptoglobin, and a negative direct antiglobulin test.
5. Bone marrow evaluation shows a hypocellular marrow without evidence of leukemia or myelodysplasia.
6. Flow cytometry demonstrates absence of CD55 and CD59 on a population of peripheral blood cells.

TEACHING_POINTS:

* Coombs-negative intravascular hemolysis with unusual venous thrombosis is a classic diagnostic pattern requiring synthesis across organ systems.
* Abdominal pain, dysphagia, and erectile dysfunction may result from nitric oxide depletion during hemolytic episodes.
* Bone marrow failure syndromes can coexist with this disorder, complicating the presentation.
* Flow cytometry for loss of GPI-anchored proteins confirms the diagnosis.

CASE_ID: 008
DIFFICULTY: hard
SYSTEM: infectious_disease

DIAGNOSIS:
Whipple disease

ALIASES:
Tropheryma whipplei infection
Intestinal lipodystrophy
Systemic Whipple disease

VIGNETTE_LINES:

1. A 55-year-old man presents with chronic diarrhea.
2. He has had months of migratory joint pains and progressive weight loss despite multiple negative stool cultures.
3. Examination shows temporal wasting and hyperpigmented skin over sun-exposed areas.
4. Laboratory studies demonstrate iron deficiency anemia and low serum albumin.
5. Upper endoscopy with small-bowel biopsy shows blunted villi containing numerous foamy macrophages in the lamina propria.
6. Polymerase chain reaction performed on the intestinal tissue detects Tropheryma whipplei.

TEACHING_POINTS:

* Chronic diarrhea with weight loss and migratory arthralgias should prompt consideration of a systemic infectious cause of malabsorption.
* Routine stool studies are often unrevealing because the disease primarily involves the small intestinal mucosa.
* Histology narrows the diagnosis, but molecular confirmation is useful because several disorders can produce macrophage-rich infiltrates.
* Delayed treatment can lead to cardiac and neurologic involvement.

CASE_ID: 009
DIFFICULTY: hard
SYSTEM: rheumatology

DIAGNOSIS:
Behçet disease

ALIASES:
Behçet syndrome
Variable vessel vasculitis of Behçet disease
Adamantiades-Behçet disease

VIGNETTE_LINES:

1. A 27-year-old man presents with recurrent mouth pain.
2. He reports repeated episodes of painful genital sores over the past year and recently developed blurry vision.
3. Examination shows multiple shallow oral aphthous ulcers and tender erythematous nodules over both shins.
4. CBC and metabolic panel are unremarkable, while inflammatory markers are mildly elevated.
5. Ophthalmologic examination demonstrates anterior uveitis with retinal vasculitis.
6. Pathergy testing produces a papulopustular lesion at the needle-stick site within 48 hours.

TEACHING_POINTS:

* Recurrent oral and genital ulcers with ocular inflammation should trigger a focused vasculitic differential rather than isolated mucocutaneous infection.
* The disease can involve arteries and veins of any size, leading to diverse vascular and neurologic manifestations.
* Erythema nodosum-like lesions and arthralgias are common but not specific, so pattern recognition is essential.
* A positive pathergy phenomenon provides supportive confirmation in the appropriate clinical context.

CASE_ID: 010
DIFFICULTY: hard
SYSTEM: pharmacology_toxicology

DIAGNOSIS:
Salicylate toxicity

ALIASES:
Aspirin poisoning
Salicylism
Acute on chronic salicylate intoxication

VIGNETTE_LINES:

1. A 46-year-old woman presents with nausea.
2. She reports taking increasing amounts of over-the-counter pain medication for chronic back pain during the past week.
3. Examination reveals tachypnea, diaphoresis, and mild confusion.
4. Arterial blood gas demonstrates a mixed respiratory alkalosis and metabolic acidosis, and basic metabolic panel shows an elevated anion gap.
5. Chest radiography reveals mild noncardiogenic pulmonary edema, and serum glucose is low-normal despite neuroglycopenic symptoms.
6. Serum toxicology testing shows a markedly elevated salicylate concentration consistent with clinically significant poisoning.

TEACHING_POINTS:

* The combination of tachypnea with a mixed acid-base disorder should strongly suggest salicylate poisoning in the right medication history.
* Toxicity may occur with repeated supratherapeutic dosing, especially when patients do not recognize that multiple products contain aspirin.
* Pulmonary edema, altered mental status, and acidemia indicate severe poisoning and need urgent management.
* Treatment centers on alkalinization and, in severe cases, hemodialysis.
