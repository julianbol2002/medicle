CASE_ID: 504
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: cardiology

DIAGNOSIS:
Wolff-Parkinson-White Syndrome

ALIASES:
WPW
pre-excitation syndrome
accessory pathway

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A young athletic man presents to the emergency department with sudden-onset palpitations and lightheadedness that began while he was jogging.
2. The episode started abruptly and resolved spontaneously after about twenty minutes before he arrived.
3. He has had two similar episodes over the past year, each resolving on their own.
4. Examination between episodes is entirely normal with no murmur and normal vital signs.
5. Resting ECG reveals a short PR interval, a slurred upstroke at the beginning of the QRS complex, and a widened QRS in multiple leads.
6. He is referred to electrophysiology for ablation of the accessory pathway.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- WPW features a delta wave (slurred QRS upstroke), short PR interval, and widened QRS due to ventricular pre-excitation via an accessory pathway.
- It can precipitate life-threatening arrhythmias including atrial fibrillation with rapid ventricular response.
- AV nodal blocking agents such as adenosine and digoxin are contraindicated in WPW with AF.

CASE_ID: 505
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: neurology

DIAGNOSIS:
Subarachnoid Hemorrhage

ALIASES:
SAH
ruptured cerebral aneurysm
subarachnoid bleed

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A middle-aged woman presents to the emergency department describing the worst headache of her life that came on suddenly while she was lifting groceries.
2. She says it felt like something exploded in the back of her head and reached maximum intensity within seconds.
3. She vomited twice en route and is now mildly confused.
4. Examination reveals nuchal rigidity, bilateral papilledema, and a left third nerve palsy with a dilated unreactive pupil.
5. Non-contrast CT of the head shows hyperdense material in the basal cisterns and sylvian fissures; lumbar puncture reveals xanthochromia with elevated red blood cells that do not decrease between tubes.
6. CT angiography identifies a saccular aneurysm at the junction of the posterior communicating artery and internal carotid artery, and she is taken for endovascular coiling.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- Subarachnoid hemorrhage classically presents with a thunderclap headache reaching peak intensity within seconds.
- A third nerve palsy with pupillary involvement suggests compression by a posterior communicating artery aneurysm.
- If CT is negative, lumbar puncture must be performed; xanthochromia confirms the diagnosis.

CASE_ID: 506
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: endocrinology

DIAGNOSIS:
Addison Disease

ALIASES:
primary adrenal insufficiency
adrenal insufficiency
Addison's disease

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A young woman presents with several months of progressive fatigue, weight loss, and intermittent nausea and vomiting.
2. She craves salty foods and feels dizzy when standing up quickly.
3. Her family has noticed a deepening tan even though she has not been in the sun, and dark patches have appeared on her gums and the creases of her palms.
4. Labs show hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, and a mild metabolic acidosis; blood glucose is low-normal.
5. Morning cortisol is low and fails to rise appropriately after synthetic ACTH stimulation; ACTH level is markedly elevated.
6. She is started on hydrocortisone and fludrocortisone replacement and counseled on sick-day dosing rules.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- Addison disease is primary adrenal insufficiency causing deficiency of both cortisol and aldosterone.
- Hyperpigmentation results from excess ACTH cross-reacting with melanocortin receptors.
- Hyponatremia and hyperkalemia distinguish primary from secondary adrenal insufficiency.

CASE_ID: 507
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: gastroenterology

DIAGNOSIS:
Hepatic Encephalopathy

ALIASES:
portosystemic encephalopathy
liver encephalopathy

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. An older man with a history of chronic liver disease is brought in by his family for confusion and inappropriate behavior over the past two days.
2. He has been more sleepy than usual and his family noticed his hands shaking in a flapping pattern.
3. He recently started a high-protein diet and has been constipated for four days.
4. Examination reveals jaundice, spider angiomata, a flapping tremor of the hands on outstretched arms, and disorientation to time and place.
5. Ammonia level is markedly elevated, EEG shows diffuse slow-wave activity, and head CT reveals no acute intracranial pathology.
6. He is started on lactulose titrated to produce several soft stools per day and oral rifaximin, with identification and treatment of a concurrent urinary tract infection as the precipitant.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- Hepatic encephalopathy results from accumulation of ammonia and other toxins due to impaired hepatic clearance.
- Asterixis (flapping tremor) is a characteristic finding.
- Common precipitants include GI bleeding, infection, constipation, excessive dietary protein, and diuretic overuse.

CASE_ID: 508
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: infectious_disease

DIAGNOSIS:
Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever

ALIASES:
RMSF
Rickettsia rickettsii infection

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A child is brought in with five days of high fever, severe headache, and myalgias that began shortly after a family camping trip.
2. The parents noticed a rash that started on the wrists and ankles two days ago and has been spreading toward the trunk.
3. One parent recalls finding a tick attached to the child's scalp on the third day of the trip.
4. Examination reveals a febrile ill-appearing child with a petechial and maculopapular rash on the palms and soles extending centrally, along with periorbital edema.
5. Labs show thrombocytopenia, hyponatremia, and elevated transaminases; the peripheral smear shows no parasites.
6. Doxycycline is started empirically without waiting for confirmatory serology, with rapid defervescence over 48 hours.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- RMSF is caused by Rickettsia rickettsii transmitted by Dermacentor ticks.
- The classic triad is fever, headache, and rash; the rash classically begins peripherally and spreads centrally.
- Doxycycline is the treatment of choice for all ages and should never be delayed pending confirmation.

CASE_ID: 509
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: nephrology

DIAGNOSIS:
Nephrotic Syndrome

ALIASES:
nephrotic syndrome unspecified
heavy proteinuria syndrome

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A middle-aged man presents with progressive bilateral leg swelling and puffiness around his eyes that has worsened over three weeks.
2. He has noticed his urine looks frothy and his pants no longer fit due to abdominal swelling.
3. He was recently diagnosed with type 2 diabetes and has poorly controlled hypertension.
4. Examination reveals pitting edema to the thighs, ascites, and periorbital edema; blood pressure is significantly elevated.
5. Urinalysis shows 4+ protein without blood, 24-hour urine protein is 6.8 grams, serum albumin is markedly low, and cholesterol is elevated; renal biopsy shows nodular glomerulosclerosis.
6. He is started on an ACE inhibitor, a diuretic for edema management, and anticoagulation given the thrombotic risk.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- Nephrotic syndrome is defined by proteinuria greater than 3.5 g/day, hypoalbuminemia, edema, and hyperlipidemia.
- Patients are at increased risk of thromboembolism due to urinary loss of antithrombin III.
- The most common cause in adults with diabetes is diabetic nephropathy.

CASE_ID: 510
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: hematology_oncology

DIAGNOSIS:
Iron Deficiency Anemia

ALIASES:
iron deficiency
IDA
microcytic anemia

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A young woman presents with several months of fatigue, exertional shortness of breath, and difficulty concentrating at work.
2. She has heavy menstrual periods lasting seven to eight days each month and has been a vegetarian for four years.
3. She reports craving and chewing ice compulsively, something she finds embarrassing.
4. Examination reveals pallor of the conjunctivae and nail beds, with spoon-shaped nails and angular cheilitis.
5. CBC shows a microcytic hypochromic anemia with elevated RDW; serum ferritin is critically low, serum iron is low, and TIBC is elevated.
6. She is started on oral ferrous sulfate and referred for gynecology evaluation of her menorrhagia.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- Iron deficiency anemia is the most common cause of anemia worldwide.
- Pica (craving non-nutritive substances, especially ice) is a classic symptom.
- Low ferritin is the most sensitive and specific test for iron deficiency.

CASE_ID: 511
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: musculoskeletal

DIAGNOSIS:
Septic Arthritis

ALIASES:
infectious arthritis
bacterial arthritis
joint infection

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A middle-aged man presents with one day of severe pain, swelling, and inability to bear weight on his right knee.
2. The joint pain came on rapidly and is associated with fever and chills.
3. He had a dental procedure four days ago and has a history of osteoarthritis in the same knee.
4. Examination reveals a warm, erythematous, markedly swollen right knee with exquisite tenderness on any range of motion; he holds the joint in slight flexion.
5. Arthrocentesis yields cloudy yellow fluid with a white cell count of 85,000 per microliter with 95% neutrophils; Gram stain reveals gram-positive cocci in clusters.
6. He is admitted for IV antibiotics and daily joint aspiration, with orthopedic surgery consulted for possible washout.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- Septic arthritis is a medical emergency requiring prompt joint aspiration and antibiotic therapy.
- Synovial fluid WBC greater than 50,000 with neutrophil predominance strongly suggests infection.
- Staphylococcus aureus is the most common cause of septic arthritis in adults.

CASE_ID: 512
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: dermatology

DIAGNOSIS:
Stevens-Johnson Syndrome

ALIASES:
SJS
SJS-TEN
erythema multiforme major

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A young woman is brought to the emergency department with a spreading painful rash, fever, and difficulty swallowing that began three days ago.
2. She started trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole for a urinary tract infection ten days prior.
3. She has developed blistering sores in her mouth, on her lips, and around her eyes that make it difficult to eat or open her eyes fully.
4. Examination reveals a toxic-appearing woman with extensive erythematous and dusky targetoid lesions across her trunk and extremities, with mucosal involvement in three distinct sites; the skin peels off with gentle lateral pressure.
5. The affected body surface area is estimated at 12%, and ophthalmology is urgently consulted for corneal involvement.
6. The offending antibiotic is immediately discontinued, she is transferred to a burn unit for wound care, and supportive management is initiated.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- Stevens-Johnson syndrome involves less than 10% BSA detachment with mucosal involvement at two or more sites.
- Drug reactions, particularly sulfonamides, anticonvulsants, and allopurinol, are the most common triggers.
- The Nikolsky sign (skin separation with lateral pressure) is positive.

CASE_ID: 513
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: reproductive

DIAGNOSIS:
Preeclampsia

ALIASES:
pre-eclampsia
pregnancy-induced hypertension with proteinuria

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A woman in her third trimester presents with a persistent frontal headache and visual disturbances including flashing lights for the past two days.
2. She has noticed significant swelling of her hands and face that came on suddenly over the past week.
3. This is her first pregnancy and she has no prior history of hypertension.
4. Blood pressure on two readings taken four hours apart is 158/102 mmHg and 162/104 mmHg; examination reveals hyperreflexia and 3+ pitting edema.
5. Urinalysis shows 3+ proteinuria, 24-hour urine protein is 400 mg, platelets are trending down, and liver enzymes are mildly elevated.
6. She is admitted, started on magnesium sulfate for seizure prophylaxis and antihypertensives, and delivery is planned given gestational age.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- Preeclampsia is defined as new-onset hypertension after 20 weeks gestation with proteinuria or end-organ dysfunction.
- Severe features include BP greater than 160/110, thrombocytopenia, renal insufficiency, liver dysfunction, or neurologic symptoms.
- Magnesium sulfate prevents progression to eclampsia; delivery is the only definitive treatment.
