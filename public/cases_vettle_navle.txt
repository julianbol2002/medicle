DIFFICULTY: navle
SYSTEM: exotic_animal_medicine
DIAGNOSIS:
Chinchilla Dental Disease
ALIASES:
Slobbers
Malocclusion
VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A 4-year-old Chinchilla presents for weight loss, reduced fecal output, and excessive drooling.
2. The fur under the chin and on the chest is wet and matted.
3. The diet consists of a pellet mix with minimal hay.
4. An oral exam under anesthesia reveals sharp enamel points on the lingual aspect of the mandibular premolars.
5. The points are filed down using a high-speed burr.
TEACHING_POINTS:
- Chinchillas are elodonts (open-rooted, continuously growing teeth).
- Lack of dietary roughage (hay) prevents normal attrition, leading to painful enamel spurs.
- Drooling ('slobbers') is the classic sign of oral pain preventing swallowing.

CASE_ID: 099
DIFFICULTY: navle
SYSTEM: exotic_animal_medicine
DIAGNOSIS:
Hedgehog Wobbly Hedgehog Syndrome
ALIASES:
WHS
Demyelinating paralysis
VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A 2-year-old African Pygmy Hedgehog presents for progressive hindlimb weakness.
2. The owner notes the hedgehog falls over constantly when trying to walk and curls into a ball less tightly.
3. Neurologic exam shows ataxia and paresis that is worse in the hindlimbs.
4. No cure exists, and supportive care (lowering food bowls, padded bedding) is initiated.
5. The hedgehog is euthanized 6 months later when it becomes tetraplegic.
TEACHING_POINTS:
- WHS is a progressive, fatal, neurodegenerative disease (demyelination) of hedgehogs.
- Signs usually begin at 2-3 years of age, starting in the hindlimbs and ascending to the forelimbs.
- Definitive diagnosis requires histopathology of the CNS post-mortem.

CASE_ID: 100
DIFFICULTY: navle
SYSTEM: avian_medicine
DIAGNOSIS:
Avian Pacheco's Disease
ALIASES:
Psittacid Herpesvirus
VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A rescue aviary experiences a sudden outbreak of acute death in several healthy Cockatoos.
2. A few birds showed yellow urates and severe diarrhea hours before dying.
3. Necropsy reveals massively enlarged, necrotic livers with petechiae.
4. Histopathology shows eosinophilic intranuclear inclusion bodies in hepatocytes.
5. The surviving exposed birds are treated with oral acyclovir.
TEACHING_POINTS:
- Pacheco's disease is a highly fatal herpesvirus of psittacine birds.
- It primarily causes acute, massive hepatic necrosis, leading to sudden death.
- Conures are known to be asymptomatic carriers and shedders of the virus.
